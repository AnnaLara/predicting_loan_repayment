Business problem

Loans for people with no credit score is an unexplored ground. Around 1 in 10 American consumers has no credit history.

On one hand, it is both a business oportunity and a chance to help people when they need it. On the other hand, a solution to decide whether the loan will be paid back is needed.


The model

Logistic Regression model that predicts the probability of default and paying back a small loan.

The data used to train the model contained only bank transaction information, no credit score involved. The data was provided by one of US startups that grants small loans to people with no credit score.


Model evaluation

Cross-validated log loss: -0.56

Cross-validated AUC score: 0.63


ROC curve


ROC curve represents the tradeof between True Positive Rate and False Positive Rate. That is, how much TPR can be increased with as little increase of the FPR as possible. If the curve is a srtaight diagonal line, the model does not have any predicting power (the case when AUC is 0.5).


Predicted and actual probability of default

For the most ranges the average true class is close to the predicted probability. This means that, for example, if a person is predicted to have 0.35 probability of default, in reality 3-4 persons out of 10 will default. These predictions can be used to estimate company's income and apply different loan conditions to individuals with higher probability of default.
